In this submission, you should present your work with a focus on the followings:

Specifically, the Process Portfolio should be a window to showcase your work. You should
cover the following content in your portfolio:
1. Motivation & Research
• The Why: A 200-word statement on why your group chose your specific track
(Heritage, Social, or Active).
• The Gap: Summarize 4 academic papers and 4 commercial products. Instead of a
summary, list 3 things they did well and 3 things they missed.
• The Stakeholders: Define primary and secondary users with distinct Personas.
2. User Requirements
• User Journey Map: A visual map showing the current pain points of your target
users.
• Requirements List: A bulleted list of 3 must-haves for the system to be considered
“Playful”.
• Evidence of Life: Upload 5 photos or short clip videos of your group interviewing a
potential user or observing the site (e.g., Suzhou Grand Canal).
3. Ideation & Alternatives
• The “Crazy Eights”: Photos of 8 rapid hand-drawn sketches of different UI ideas.
• Design Alternatives: Compare at least 2-3 different ways the system could work and
explain why you chose one over the other(s).
• Low-Fi Prototype: A link to a clickable Figma prototype.
4. Technical Implementation
• System Architecture: A diagram showing how your Web App handles data.
• High-Fi Prototype: Ideally a URL to the hosted system (e.g., yourproject.github.io or
yourproject.vercel.app).
• Individual Contributions: A table showing exactly what each of the 4 members built
(Code, UI Design, Content, or Testing, etc.).
5. Evaluation & Reflection
• Usability Testing: Results from testing your “Alpha” version with 3 real people.
• Iterative Refinement: “Before and After” screenshots showing a change you made
based on user feedback.
• Final Reflection: A brief discussion on the social/ethical implications of your design,
and the use of AI in this project (check more details in the Generative AI Permissions
section).
